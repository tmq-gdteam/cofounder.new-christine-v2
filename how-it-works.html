How it works

Your goal becomes visible, approval-gated work.

cofounder.new is designed around a founder loop: direction in, structured workstreams out, with clear review points where judgment or permission is needed.

Request early accessSee the control layer

Step walkthrough

Six steps from intent to progress.

The mechanics stay bounded and reviewable. The system is presented as an execution layer, not as a promise of unsupervised company building.

  1. Intake

    Capture the founder's current goal, constraints, and open questions in plain language.

  2. Priorities

    Turn the goal into a few workstreams with a clear next decision.

  3. Specialist work

    Prepare drafts, research notes, product outlines, or operating summaries as conceptual outputs.

  4. Approvals

    Hold sensitive moves until the founder approves, edits, or rejects them.

  5. Output

    Log approved work into a visible trail so useful context does not vanish.

  6. Progress report

    Show what changed, what is waiting, and what deserves attention next.

Automatic vs. needs you

The boundary is part of the product.

cofounder.new should make it obvious which work can be summarized internally and which work requires explicit founder permission.

Can move as internal work

Drafting, summarizing, organizing, comparing options, and preparing next-step recommendations can be shown as internal progress.

Needs founder approval

Spending, publishing, emailing, code changes, and public commitments should be held for review before action.

Answer-style clarity

Common questions, plain answers.

The site avoids broad reassurance and answers the safety objection directly.

What is an AI cofounder?

It is a positioning metaphor for an execution layer that helps a founder turn direction into visible work.

What does it do?

It is designed to structure founder intent into workstreams, drafts, approval points, and progress summaries.
